How to Remove Hair With Laser Treatment

Laser hair removal is an effective and popular method for removing hair from the head. The device uses a laser to disintegrate hair follicles, leaving the skin clear and free of hair.


There are a variety of different types of lasers used in laser hair removal: excimer lasers, femtosecond lasers, pulsed light ablation (PLA), or PDT. Excimer lasers use extremely high-energy pulses to destroy the follicles. Femtosecond lasers use shorter pulses that target specific areas of the follicle. PLAs work by using multiple short bursts of energy to destroy the cells within a follicle. PDT uses a pulsed light beam to destroy the cell's torch this way.

How to Apply the Laser Treatment

Before beginning laser hair removal, it’s important to determine how much hair you want removed. The size of your target area will dictate how many treatments you need per day – larger areas will require more sessions per day, while smaller areas will only require a few treatments each day. You should also allow for at least four days between treatments in order to allow your skin time to heal and restore lost hair growth.
